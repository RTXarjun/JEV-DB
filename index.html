Japanese Encephalitis Virus (JEV) is a mosquito-borne flavivirus that primarily affects humans and animals in Asia and the Western Pacific. It is the leading cause of viral encephalitis in these regions, with pigs and wading birds serving as reservoir hosts. JEV is transmitted through the bite of infected Culex mosquitoes, particularly Culex tritaeniorhynchus. Most infections are asymptomatic, but severe cases can lead to brain inflammation, seizures, paralysis, and even death. Vaccination and mosquito control are the primary measures to prevent the spread of JEV.
